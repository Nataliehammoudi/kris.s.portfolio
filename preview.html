Kris Azzeh
A character study in one act · University of Jordan · Draft 23

(Lights rise slowly. A young theater student stands between page and stage.)

Kris

I grew up believing theater was the only room where telling the truth was not only allowed but required. Everything else felt like rehearsal for something that never quite arrived.

the honest version

(A beat. Kris adjusts. Something like honesty, finally.)

Kris

I study at the University of Jordan, where I've learned that a stage isn't a place you stand on. It's a platform contract between you and whoever's watching. You owe them something real.

Kris

I've played villains who made me nervous. I've played characters who said things I could never say as myself. I've stood in the dark before a curtain lifted and thought: this is the most alive I will feel today.

(Kris looks directly at the audience. No pretense left.)

Kris

I'm still in rehearsal. That's the point. Theater taught me that perfection presence is the only thing worth working toward.

(Lights fade. End of scene.)
