The Performer

My name is Kris Azzeh. I am a theater student at the University of Jordan, where I study performance, dramatic theory, stagecraft, and the craft of character work. Theater, for me, is not a subject. It is a way of being fully present in the world.

I am passionate about performance, the art of inhabiting another person so completely that the boundary between self and character dissolves in front of an audience. I believe great acting begins not in the voice or the body, but in genuine emotional truth.

Storytelling drives everything I do. Whether on a stage, behind a camera, or in a rehearsal room, I am always asking: what does this story need? What does it demand from the person telling it?

"The stage is not a place you walk onto. It is a place you become."

I am drawn to stage presence, the invisible force that makes an audience lean in without knowing why. I study it, practise it, and am humbled by it daily. I am also committed to the collaborative nature of theater, the ensemble, the director, the playwright, the light designer, all of it a single organism telling one truth.

I am based in Amman, Jordan. I am open to productions, collaborations, workshops, and conversations.
