About

A performer who believes the stage is the most honest place a person can stand.

My name is Kris Azzeh. I am a Theater student at the University of Jordan, where I study performance, dramatic theory, stagecraft, and the full language of the stage.


I am drawn to storytelling in its most raw form. To the moment an audience holds its breath without knowing why. To the silence between two lines of dialogue that carries more weight than the words themselves.

"Character work is not about becoming someone else. It is about finding every version of yourself that already exists."

I work with performance, movement, voice, and character analysis. I am passionate about the emotional power of theater, its ability to hold a mirror to the world and ask the audience to look back at themselves.


Based in Amman, Jordan. Open to productions, workshops, collaborations, and creative conversations.

The Craft

Skills

"Every skill below is a discipline, not a checkbox. Each one is practised, studied, and tested in front of a live audience."

01
Acting

The core discipline. Inhabiting truth under imaginary circumstances. Emotional honesty on demand.

02
Stage Presence

The invisible force that holds a room. Studied, practised, and never fully mastered.

03
Improvisation

Listening as an art form. The courage to respond truthfully without preparation.

04
Voice Control

Pitch, resonance, breath, projection. The voice as instrument, not just speech.

05
Movement

Physical theater, spatial awareness, and the body as a storytelling tool.

06
Character Analysis

Reading a character's history, psychology, and desire before walking on stage.

07
Script Reading

Dramaturgical understanding of text, subtext, rhythm, and structure.

08
Teamwork

Theater is never solo. The ensemble, the director, the crew, all are equal authors.

09
Performance Storytelling

Shaping a narrative arc within a performance. Making the audience feel the story, not just see it.
